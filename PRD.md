# **Problem Statement**

Modern people consume enormous amounts of information but rarely train the underlying skills needed to use it effectively.

Most struggle with

* poor focus  
* distraction  
* forgetfulness  
* emotional reactions  
* bad decisions  
* low productivity  
* weak learning habits

Existing brain-training apps mostly provide games that rarely translate into everyday life.

BrainGym fills this gap.

# **MVP Roadmap**

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):** Authentication, onboarding, profiles, activity library, daily workouts, progress tracking, reminders, XP, streaks.

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):** AI Coach, Decision Lab, reports, achievements, leaderboards.

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):** Community challenges, Brain Buddy, referrals, premium subscriptions.

**Phase 4:** School and corporate editions, family mode, marketplace, wearable integrations.

## **One strategic improvement**

After reading the entire document, I believe the biggest opportunity is to shift the product from **"89 activities"** to **"Life Missions."** Your later notes already hint at this direction.

Imagine opening BrainGym and choosing:

* Get promoted at work  
* Become a better communicator  
* Pass my professional exam  
* Build a successful business  
* Improve my memory  
* Become more disciplined  
* Become emotionally resilient

The app then generates daily brain workouts specifically for that mission. Instead of feeling like a habit tracker, BrainGym becomes a personal mental performance coach that helps users achieve meaningful life outcomes. I believe that positioning is much stronger, easier to market, and harder for competitors to copy.
